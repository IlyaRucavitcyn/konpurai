Vendor Management

The Vendor Management system serves as a centralized location to track all external entities that provide AI-related products, services, or components to your organization. This is a core compliance component for the VerifyWise platform, enabling you to maintain oversight of your vendor relationships and associated risks.

Overview

The Vendor Management page consists of two main tabs:

  • Vendors Tab: Manage vendor information, review status, and assignments
  • Risks Tab: Track and manage vendor-specific risks with automated risk calculations

Vendors Tab

The Vendors tab displays a comprehensive table of all vendors with the following information:

  • Name: The vendor's company or organization name
  • Assignee: The team member responsible for managing this vendor relationship
  • Status: Current review status (Not started, In review, Reviewed, Requires follow-up)
  • Risk: Overall risk assessment for the vendor
  • Review Date: When the vendor was last reviewed or when the next review is due

Adding New Vendors

To add a new vendor to your system:

  1. Click the "Add new vendor" button in the top right corner
  2. Fill in the required fields:
    • Vendor Name: The official name of the vendor company (required, max 64 characters)
    • Website: The vendor's official website URL (required, max 64 characters)
    • Projects: Select one or more projects this vendor is associated with (required)
    • Vendor Contact Person: Primary contact person at the vendor (required, max 64 characters)
    • Assignee: Team member responsible for this vendor relationship (required)
    • What does the vendor provide?: Detailed description of services/products (required, max 64 characters)
    • Review Status: Current status of vendor review (required)
    • Reviewer: Team member conducting the vendor review (required)
    • Review Date: Date of the review (defaults to current date)
    • Review Result: Summary of the review findings (required, max 64 characters)
  3. Click "Save" to create the vendor

Review Status Options

Vendors can have one of the following review statuses:

  • Not started: Vendor review has not yet begun
  • In review: Vendor is currently being evaluated
  • Reviewed: Vendor review has been completed
  • Requires follow-up: Additional action is needed after initial review

Managing Vendors

For each vendor in the table, you can:

  • Edit: Click the actions menu (three dots) to edit vendor details
  • Delete: Use the actions menu to remove a vendor (requires confirmation and appropriate permissions)
  • Filter by Project: Use the project filter dropdown to view vendors by specific project

Risks Tab

The Risks tab provides comprehensive vendor risk management with the following features:

  • Risk Summary Cards: Overview of risk distribution across vendors
  • Detailed Risk Table: Complete list of all vendor risks with filtering capabilities
  • Automated Risk Calculation: Risk levels are automatically calculated based on likelihood and severity

Adding New Vendor Risks

To add a new risk associated with a vendor:

  1. Navigate to the "Risks" tab
  2. Click the "Add new Risk" button
  3. Fill in the required fields:
    • Vendor: Select the vendor this risk is associated with (required)
    • Action Owner: Team member responsible for managing this risk (required)
    • Risk Description: Detailed description of the risk (required, max 256 characters)
    • Impact Description: Description of potential impact if risk occurs (required, max 256 characters)
    • Action Plan: Steps to mitigate or manage this risk (required, max 256 characters)
  4. Calculate Risk Level: Use the risk calculation section to determine risk level:
    • Likelihood: How likely is this risk to occur? (Rare, Unlikely, Possible, Likely, Almost Certain)
    • Severity: How severe would the impact be? (Negligible, Minor, Moderate, Major, Catastrophic)
    • Risk Level: Automatically calculated based on likelihood × severity matrix
  5. Click "Save" to create the risk

Risk Level Calculation

The system automatically calculates risk levels using a 5×5 matrix combining likelihood and severity:

  • Very High Risk: High likelihood with major/catastrophic severity, or almost certain likelihood with moderate+ severity
  • High Risk: Likely occurrence with moderate+ severity, or possible occurrence with major+ severity
  • Medium Risk: Possible occurrence with moderate severity, or unlikely occurrence with major+ severity
  • Low Risk: Unlikely occurrence with minor/moderate severity, or rare occurrence with moderate+ severity
  • Very Low Risk: Rare occurrence with negligible/minor severity, or unlikely occurrence with negligible severity

Risk Management Features

For each risk in the table, you can:

  • Edit: Click on a risk row or use the actions menu to edit risk details
  • Delete: Use the actions menu to remove a risk (requires confirmation and appropriate permissions)
  • Filter by Project: Use the project filter to view risks by specific project
  • Filter by Vendor: Use the vendor filter to view risks for a specific vendor

Risk Table Columns

The risk table displays the following information:

  • Risk Description: Brief description of the risk
  • Vendor: Associated vendor name
  • Project: Project(s) affected by this risk (with tooltip for multiple projects)
  • Action Owner: Team member responsible for managing this risk
  • Risk Severity: Severity level with color-coded chip
  • Likelihood: Probability of risk occurrence
  • Risk Level: Calculated overall risk level with color-coded chip

Color Coding

Risk levels and severities use consistent color coding:

  • Green: Very low risk, Negligible severity
  • Light Green: Low risk, Minor severity
  • Brown: Medium risk, Moderate severity
  • Orange: High risk, Major severity
  • Red: Very high risk, Catastrophic severity

Permissions and Access Control

Access to vendor management features is controlled by user roles:

  • Create: Only users with vendor creation permissions can add new vendors or risks
  • Edit: Only users with vendor edit permissions can modify existing vendors or risks
  • Delete: Only users with vendor delete permissions can remove vendors or risks

If you don't have the necessary permissions, the "Add new vendor" or "Add new Risk" buttons will be disabled, and edit/delete options will not be available in the actions menu.

Best Practices

  • Regular Reviews: Schedule regular vendor reviews to ensure compliance and risk assessment accuracy
  • Clear Risk Descriptions: Provide detailed, specific risk descriptions to enable effective mitigation planning
  • Action Plans: Always include concrete, actionable steps in your risk mitigation plans
  • Project Association: Ensure vendors are properly associated with relevant projects for accurate filtering and reporting
  • Assignee Management: Keep vendor and risk assignees up-to-date to ensure proper accountability
